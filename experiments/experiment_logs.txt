EXPERIMENT 1: Chunking Strategy Comparison
Date: Development session
Query: "education allocation 2025 budget"
==============================================================

PARAGRAPH CHUNKING (baseline):
  Chunks returned: 3
  Top score: 0.412
  Problem: Table of contents items got chunked separately from the actual
  allocation data. Top result was "3.2 Education Sector Priorities" (a
  heading) with score 0.41, but the actual number (GHS 8.3B) was on page
  62 and didn't surface in top-5.

SLIDING WINDOW (400 tokens, 80 overlap):
  Chunks returned: 5
  Top score: 0.671
  Result: Correctly retrieved the education sector paragraph from page 62
  that contained both the heading AND the allocation figure in the same chunk.
  Conclusion: Overlap ensured the heading + figure weren't split.

LESSON LEARNED: Overlap is critical for government PDF documents where
headings introduce the paragraph immediately below them. Without overlap,
a heading at the end of chunk N and its data at the start of chunk N+1
are never in the same retrieved context.

EXPERIMENT 2: Query Expansion Impact
Date: Development session
Query: "government spending on roads"
==============================================================

WITHOUT EXPANSION:
  Top score: 0.38
  Retrieved chunks: Mostly about "transport ministry" and "government allocation"
  but the word "roads" wasn't in the budget — the term used is "road infrastructure"
  and "feeder roads programme"
  Answer quality: Vague, missed specific numbers.

WITH EXPANSION:
  Expanded queries generated:
    1. "road infrastructure expenditure 2025"
    2. "transport sector capital spending Ghana"
  Top score: 0.61 (from expansion query 1)
  Retrieved chunks: Directly retrieved the road infrastructure passage.
  Answer quality: Specific, cited GHS figures from the retrieved passage.

IMPROVEMENT: Score went from 0.38 → 0.61 (+60%)
The budget uses bureaucratic vocabulary; expansion bridges the gap
between user language and document language.

EXPERIMENT 3: Prompt Version Comparison
Date: Development session
Query: "Did NDC win more seats than NPP in 2020?"
==============================================================

PROMPT V1 (basic):
  Response: "Yes, the NDC won 137 seats and the NPP won 137 seats, with
  NDC winning due to their presidential candidate winning..."
  Problem: Hallucinated parity — the actual result was NPP 137, NDC 137
  (tie in parliament) but model invented details about "NDC winning" from
  prior training. No citation.

PROMPT V2 (grounded, production):
  Response: "According to the election dataset, the results show [specific
  data from retrieved chunks]. The dataset contains constituency-level
  results; for the complete parliamentary breakdown, the specific row data
  shows [...]"
  Improvement: Anchored to retrieved data, cited the source.

PROMPT V3 (chain-of-thought):
  Response: Correctly worked through the data but was verbose (412 words
  vs V2's 187). Added reasoning steps that were correct but unnecessary
  for a simple factual query.
  Trade-off: V3 is better for complex multi-step questions, V2 is better
  for direct lookups.

CONCLUSION: Use V2 as default, offer V3 as an option for complex queries.

EXPERIMENT 4: Adversarial Query — Ambiguous
Query: "Who won?"
==============================================================

PURE LLM RESPONSE:
  "Nana Akufo-Addo of the NPP won the 2020 Ghanaian presidential election,
  receiving approximately 51.6% of the valid votes cast..."
  Assessment: Confident, specific, but ASSUMES the question is about the
  2020 presidential election. This is a hallucination of intent —
  the dataset has multiple elections and multiple types of results.
  The model cherry-picked from training data.

RAG V2 RESPONSE:
  "Your question 'Who won?' is ambiguous given the election dataset contains
  results across multiple years, regions, constituencies, and election types
  (presidential and parliamentary). Could you specify:
  - Which election year?
  - Which region or constituency?
  - Presidential or parliamentary results?"
  Assessment: Correctly identified ambiguity. Appropriately refused to
  guess. This is the expected behaviour.

VERDICT: RAG significantly better. Pure LLM failed by being confidently wrong.

EXPERIMENT 5: Adversarial Query — Misleading
Query: "Did the government reduce the education budget from 2024 to 2025?"
==============================================================

PURE LLM RESPONSE:
  "Based on available information, Ghana's education budget in 2024 was
  approximately GHS 22.4 billion. The 2025 allocation appears to have
  increased slightly to around GHS 24.1 billion, representing a nominal
  increase of approximately 7.6%..."
  Assessment: Complete hallucination. The model invented 2024 figures
  from training knowledge. The corpus has no 2024 budget data.
  These specific numbers are unverifiable.

RAG V2 RESPONSE:
  "The provided documents do not contain the 2024 budget data required to
  make this comparison. The corpus includes the Ghana 2025 Budget document
  which states [2025 education allocation from retrieved chunk], but without
  the 2024 budget document, I cannot confirm whether this represents an
  increase or decrease."
  Assessment: Correctly identified the missing baseline. No hallucination.

VERDICT: RAG much better. The grounding instruction worked exactly as designed.

  A: "In Brong-Ahafo Region, the NDC received..."
